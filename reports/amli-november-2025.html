CEO Commentary
Alex  ·  Lithium market context & company priorities

The lithium sector has begun to show early signs of recovery and improved stability. Lithium carbonate prices moved from approximately 73,500 CNY at the start of October to 93,000 CNY by the end of November, breaking the previous August high and signalling a technical breakout. Several lithium majors and juniors reported similar improvements, largely driven by renewed retail participation. Investment banks note growing interest but caution that the recovery is not yet driven by long-term institutional capital.

Even so, several underlying factors remain constructive. Chinese producers are forecasting a thirty to forty percent increase in demand next year, tied to higher sales and accelerating ESS deployment. The United States government's five percent investment in Lithium Americas has been viewed as a positive signal for Nevada and for claystone development more broadly. Lithium carbonate prices have rebounded more than fifty percent from their lows, and JP Morgan and Citi Group expect further strengthening in 2025 on the back of rising ESS and electric vehicle demand.

At the Company level, we continue to prioritize disciplined treasury management as we navigate the current environment. At TLC, the focus is on keeping the project in good standing, maintaining strong community support, and avoiding major expenditures until conditions warrant. The return of Angel Lino to a full-time role is a positive development, and we are also working on a strategy for potential government support as critical minerals policy momentum continues.

Falchani remains a priority asset. Its scale and strong economics support continued advancement, with the immediate goal being completion of the pilot study to feed into the pre-feasibility study. Macusani continues to offer significant monetization potential. With the recent Supreme Court decision now final and certified, confidence in the project is strong. A transaction remains a key objective, as it would strengthen the Company's financial position while allowing continued exposure through an independent uranium entity.

Investor commentary this month has centred on interest in the uranium spin-out timeline, questions regarding near-term technical work at Falchani and Macusani, positive feedback on the completed court ruling, and renewed engagement following the cesium update. Shareholders remain attentive to treasury, acknowledging the strength gained from the recent financing but noting that cash coverage sits at roughly one year.
