CEO Commentary
Alex  ·  Lithium market context & company priorities

The lithium sector has continued to strengthen through year end and into January 2026, showing clearer signs of recovery and improved stability. Lithium carbonate prices, which were approximately 88,500 CNY at the end of November, increased to roughly 153,500 CNY by the end of January, equating to approximately US $22,175 per tonne. This represents a significant rebound from late 2025 lows and reinforces the improving price environment. Several lithium majors and juniors have reported similar sentiment improvements, supported by renewed retail participation and early signs of broader capital re-engagement. While institutional capital has not yet fully returned to the sector, momentum continues to build.

Underlying fundamentals remain constructive. The CEO of Ganfeng recently indicated that lithium spot prices could approach US $30,000 per tonne this year as the market transitions from oversupply to tighter conditions in 2026. Lithium juniors have begun recapitalizing under the improved price stability, signaling renewed confidence across the sector. In the United States, commitments to establish a potential price floor for critical minerals have been viewed as an additional structural tailwind, particularly for domestically exposed lithium projects.

At the Company level, the primary focus remains the immediate advancement of Falchani through completion of the pilot program, which will support progression toward the pre-feasibility study. In parallel, advancing the uranium spin-out of Macusani remains a key strategic objective. With the Supreme Court decision now final and certified, the project is positioned for structural separation intended to unlock value and strengthen the Company's balance sheet.

Investor commentary this month increasingly focused on the uranium spin-out, with growing pressure for clarity on timing and next steps. Interest in the strength of the Company's lithium assets also increased, alongside broadly positive feedback on recent management changes. Shareholders continue to recognize the strength of the Company's treasury following the recent financing, while also questioning the sale of the Surge position. Sentiment has shifted to acknowledge the strength of the remaining warrant position and the optionality it provides.
